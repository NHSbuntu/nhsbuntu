An operating system designed for the NHS

Secure, modern, affordable, open source, and free - it's the solution to rising licensing costs and replacing creaking NHS tech infrastructure

NHSbuntu is a free, Ubuntu-based Linux operating system for the NHS. It's intended to be a free OS that can work in both clinical and non-clinical NHS settings, and comes with the right stuff to be productive out of the box.

Our Ethos

We understand that the needs of healthcare differ from other types of enterprise, and have built NHSbuntu accordingly. We know that the NHS delivers healthcare in an unique way, which is why we've set up NHSbuntu to be more bothered with productivity than billing

Medicine, as a profession, has always understood the need to share information and learning so that the next generation of medicine advances from this one. We believe that this should include the software that we use to deliver medical care. Open source software is a way to share and build on the best, and provide every NHS institution with the very latest and best
